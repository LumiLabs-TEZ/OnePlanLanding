From the videos you save to a real trip with friends

By the OnePlan team·June 2026·6 min read

It is 2am and you are still scrolling. A café with sunlight pouring through bamboo blinds. A mountain pass swallowed by cloud. An empty beach someone filmed in a hurry on their phone. Your thumb keeps tapping save, save, save, and the same quiet promise plays in your head: we have to go there.

Then morning comes and the saved folder just sits there. Next to hundreds of other videos from other sleepless nights. Beautiful, full of wanderlust, and going precisely nowhere.

Everyone in the group has a folder like this

You are not the only one. Your best friend has the exact same wall of Reels. The one who always suggests a trip has a TikTok saved tab a mile long. Every few weeks the group chat lights up, "let's go", "let's actually book it", "are we free next month", and then goes dark again. The folder stays a folder.

The problem is not a lack of ideas

The group is not short on places to go. The problem is the opposite: too many, scattered everywhere. Dozens of clips in your TikTok saves, a few Instagram collections, a pile of screenshots, a handful of links dropped into the group chat and lost by lunchtime. Nobody turns the inspiration into a plan, so it stays inspiration.

We save so much and travel so rarely. The gap between the two is not money or time, it is a plan nobody has started.

Start with the videos themselves

The trick is to not let the videos grow old in the saved tab. Pull them out. With OnePlan you just save or share a TikTok or Instagram link into the app, and it reads the café, the homestay, the viewpoint hiding inside the clip and pins it straight onto a map. Your 2am daydream suddenly has coordinates.

When the whole group pins to one map

The moment it clicks is when everyone drops their saved spots onto one shared map. Suddenly you can see the trip. The cluster near the lake becomes day one. The pin nobody remembers saving becomes the best story of the whole trip. The group stops arguing in the chat and starts pointing at a map.

Split the days, split the bill, then just go

The rest is light. Drag each pin into a day-by-day plan, add times, give every day a "day lead". Log who paid for what as you go, and at the end OnePlan works it out and settles up for the group in a single tap. The dull parts handled, so all that is left is the fun.

And then, the café from the video...

One afternoon you push open a glass door and there it is. The exact café from the Reel you saved at 2am. The light still falls through the bamboo just like the video. Except this time your friends are beside you, laughing, ordering the wrong thing, taking the photos that will end up in someone else's saved folder.

The screen finally became a place. The videos you save were never meant to be forgotten, they are a trip waiting to be planned.
