Seoul in 4 friendly days

By the OnePlan team·Jun 2026·7 stops · 4 days

Palaces in the morning, hanok lanes by lunch, night markets after dark, and the river to slow it all down. Seoul is easy to love and easy to get around.

Scroll to follow the four days, the map tracks each stop along the way.

Day 1 · Palaces & lanes
1

Gyeongbokgung

Morning

Begin at the grand palace; rent a hanbok nearby and entry is free. Time it for the changing-of-the-guard if you can.

2

Bukchon Hanok Village

Late morning

Wander the hillside lanes of traditional houses, quiet, photogenic, and still lived-in, so tread softly.

Day 2 · City & views
3

Myeongdong

Afternoon

Skincare, street snacks and crowds, the city's shopping engine. Come for the tornado potato, stay for the people-watching.

4

N Seoul Tower

Sunset

Ride up Namsan for the whole city at golden hour; leave a love-lock if that's your thing.

Day 3 · Young Seoul
5

Hongdae

Evening

The student quarter, buskers, tiny bars, and the best night-out energy in the city.

Day 4 · Food & river
6

Gwangjang Market

Morning

Breakfast like a local: bindaetteok, mayak gimbap, and live octopus if you're feeling brave.

7

Han River · Yeouido

Afternoon

End by the river, rent a mat, grab convenience-store ramyeon, and watch the city slow down.
